# Academic Research Question

**Research Question:**
How can interactive digital storytelling experiences that integrate augmented reality (AR) technology with cultural heritage narratives increase community engagement and mental well-being among urban populations aged 18-35 in post-Celtic Tiger Ireland?

**Sub-questions:**
1. What role does cultural nostalgia (rooted in mythology and folklore) play in fostering emotional connection to place among diaspora and urban youth?
2. How can embodied digital interactions (gesture-based, movement-responsive) enhance the affective memory encoding of cultural stories?
3. What are the measurable impacts of participatory AR cultural experiences on perceived social connectedness and anxiety reduction?

**Theoretical Framework:**
Drawing from embodied cognition theory, cultural sustainability studies, and the uses and gratifications model, this study examines how magical-realist narratives (exemplified by folklore elements such as the "Dancing Cat of Dublin") can be technologically mediated to create meaningful civic experiences.

**Methodology:**
Mixed-methods approach combining:
- Quantitative surveys measuring well-being pre/post experience
- Qualitative interviews exploring narrative immersion and cultural identity
- Eye-tracking and biometric data during AR interactions
- Community focus groups in Dublin, Cork, and Galway

**Keywords:** Augmented Reality, Cultural Heritage, Ireland, Community Well-being, Digital Storytelling, Embodied Interaction, Celtic Mythology, Social Connection
